## Overview
Communities are one of the best ways to learn cybersecurity because they provide discussion, collaboration, shared tools, event information, and access to current ideas in the field. This page highlights communities that are usefull for security learning, red teaming, and professional development.

## Featured Communities

### OWASP
A community focused on improving software security.

** Why it matters: **
OWASP is one of the most recognized security communities and is especially useful for web application security, security development, and learning common vulnerabilities.

### DEF CON Forums
A community for people interested in hacking and security reasearch to meet and discuss ideas.

** Why it matters:**
This is useful for seeing what people in the hacker community are interested in and how they talk about tools, events, and techniques.

### FIRST
A community for security and incident response professionals to share information.

** Why it matters:**
FIRST is especially valuable for understanding collaboration in incident response and threat sharing.

### Red Team Village
A community focused on red teaming and offensive security.

** Why it matters:**
This is one of the most directly relevant communities for offensive security learning and red team content.

### Infosec.Exchange
A community centered around information security.

** Why it matters:**
This is a good place to follow conversations, researchers, and security topics in a more social format.

## Good Starting Point

For someone new to this space, OWASP and Red Team Village are strong starting points because they combine learning resources with an active security community.
